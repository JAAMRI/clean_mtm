Yiayia’s Greek Pastitsio (Lasagna)
Recipe: Made with Love
By Tania Motuzas, @taniamotuzas

“Pastitsio is traditional Greek comfort food. Pasta layered with meat, cheese and a creamy Béchamel sauce that’s baked together to form Lasagna. This recipe is one of my childhood favourites, a specialty of my Yiayia, Kiki (which means Grandmother in Greek). It’s been in the family for a very long time, I have added a few minor tweaks to make it my own!”

“My Yiayia, Kiki had a restaurant in Montreal (as many Greek’s do) and her customers used to call her ‘mama’ even though she was in her 30’s because she would feed everyone like a Mother. Growing up she would take the train to come visit us every summer. It was always such an exciting time because she would have one suitcase with her personal belongings and another full of food. She would pack it tightly with feta and olives from the specialty grocer. Montreal smoked meat with a fresh loaf of rye bread would also make an appearance and of course freshly baked cookies and baklava. Yiayia is such a selfless woman, to this day, she brings food to families who are less fortunate and always cooks with love. This recipe means so much to me because she taught me how to make it just the way she does. Every bite is like a warm hug that bring me back to my childhood.”

Yiayia's Greek Pastitsio Recipe
8 Servings
Prep Time: 20 minutes Cook Time:1 hour 30 minutes
450g 1lb. penne pasta or bucatini or ziti
15 mL 3 tsp. olive oil, divided
450 g 1 lbs. ground beef
1 medium onion, chopped
5 mL 1 tsp. dried oregano leaves
5 mL 1 tsp. cinnamon
1 can (794 g) whole peeled tomatoes (preferable San Marzano)
1 package Knorr® Bechamel Classic Sauce Mix
500 mL 2 cups 1% milk
250 mL 1 cups grated Parmesan cheese, divided
5 mL 1 tsp. ground nutmeg

Method

Preparing Pastitsio requires three basic steps before assembling and placing the dish into the oven: preparing the meat sauce, then the pasta, and lastly the Béchamel sauce. In 20 minutes, the Pastitsio will be ready to place into the oven.

1
Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C). Cook pasta according to package directions for al dente. Drain; return to pot and toss with 2 tsp. (10 mL) olive oil. Cover and set aside.
2
While pasta cooks, make meat sauce. Heat remaining 1 tsp. (5 mL)olive oil in large nonstick skillet and cook ground beef and onions, stirring occasionally, until beef is browned. Stir in oregano, cinnamon (which is what really set's this dish off and gives it the truly nostalgic Pastitsio flavour. It may sound weird but trust me on this one) and then salt and pepper to taste, if desired. Stir in tomatoes and bring to a boil, breaking up tomatoes with a wooden spoon. Reduce heat and simmer 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat and set aside.


3
Make the Bechamel Sauce: Bechamel can be tricky to make which is why I like to use the Knorr Bechamel Classic Sauce Mix. Whisk Knorr® Bechamel Classic Sauce Mix and 1% milk in medium saucepan until blended. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, whisking constantly. Reduce heat and simmer 3 minutes, whisking frequently until thickened. Stir in 1 cup parmesan cheese and nutmeg.
4
Time to assemble the dish. I like to spread about 1/2 cup (125 mL) meat sauce onto bottom of deep 13 x 10-in. (3.5 to 4 L) baking dish. Combine pasta and remaining meat sauce and pour into baking dish, then pour bechamel sauce over pasta to cover. Sprinkle remaining 1/2 cup (125 mL) Parmesan evenly over top.
5

Bake 45 to 60 minutes, or until the top is golden brown. Remove from oven and let stand 15 minutes before cutting. Enjoy!

The real question is when to make this dish. I like to make it for a Sunday night dinner with friends. I’ll whip up a Greek salad to go with it, some warm crusty bread and a bottle of red wine. If there are any leftovers it freezes well which is great for a lazy evening when you don’t feel like cooking. I’ll wrap individual pieces in saran wrap and place in the freezer.
