“With all that food and travel inspiration found at mealsthatmatter.com , it was a true honour when I was also asked to share one of my own personal favourite recipes that has been a staple in my family.”

“Eggs have always been one of my favourite things to eat. Whether it comes poached, scrambled, or sunny-side-up, I welcome it served every which way. Growing up in a family that always loved entertaining guests, “Deviled Eggs” were almost always a dish that my mom would make every time we had anyone over. I think that’s probably why I loved having guests over all the time as a kid because it was almost a guarantee that a plate of Deviled Eggs would be served up on our dining table. And as to be expected, before the night was over, I would have indulged in at least a large handful all by myself!”

“The classic deviled eggs recipe is quite simple as it consists of really just a hard-boiled egg and mayonnaise. I just absolutely love the creamy egg texture piped onto the egg vessel, and nothing beats the simplicity of finger food. The trick that I’ve learned to get that perfect egg mixture is to use quality mayonnaise. I use Hellman’s Real Mayonnaise that are made with only cage-free eggs. Hellman’s is the only brand of mayonnaise you’ll find in my fridge actually. It’s the quality I trust and is the magic ingredient to making the best Deviled Eggs possible.”

“In making this dish my own, I wanted to put a twist on the classic Deviled Eggs recipe and add some pizazz and personality to elevate the dish to the next level. My “Beet & Avocado Deviled Eggs” uses cooked beets and fresh avocados to not only inject some colour into the dish, but add some great new textures and flavours to the creamy egg mixture that we all have come to know and love. These Beet & Avocado Deviled Eggs are the perfect bite size appetizers to serve up to your guests, and from personal experience they will all be gone before you even know it!”

Beet & Avacado Deviled Eggs
8 Servings
Prep Time: 15 minutes Cook Time:30 minutes
8 hard cooked eggs, halved lengthwise, yolks removed and set aside
1 cooked red beet
60mL 1/4 cup Hellmann's® Real Mayonnaise
15 mL 1Tbsp olive oil
1 avocado, peeled and pitted
2 cloves peeled garlic
1
Blend egg yolks, beets, Hellmann's® Real Mayonnaise and olive oil in blender or food processor until smooth and creamy, adding a little water if needed. Season with salt and pepper, if desired. Remove to bowl and rinse blender or food processor.
2
Blend avocado, garlic, and salt to taste, if desired, in blender or food processor and until smooth.
3
Transfer egg and beet mixture to zip-close food storage bag. Snip off one corner and pipe into egg halves. Transfer avocado mixture into another bag; snip off one corner then pipe on top of the beet mixture. Enjoy!
