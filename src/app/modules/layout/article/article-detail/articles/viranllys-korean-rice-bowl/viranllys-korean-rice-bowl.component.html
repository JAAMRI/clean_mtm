Find Adventure in Food with
my Korean Rice Bowl
By @viranlly

“For most of us, at this day and age, travelling the world is just a fading memory that we’re holding on oh so dearly. The experience of discovering new things, eating and drinking at new places, meeting strangers have now turned into intimate gatherings with our closest friends, sharing comforting meals, in the familiar setting that is our home. Which, honestly, is not a bad thing.”

“Food is and can be a powerful tool that triggers and alters our minds and brings us back to the great times we’ve experienced in the past.”

“I created this Korean Rice Bowl dish to recreate memories of sharing delicious food in the lively K-Town of Los Angeles. Not only that Korean barbecue is delicious and drool-worthy, there’s something about cooking your meat in one sizzling-hot grill (while bumping loud music playing in the background too) that brings so much joy. With this dish, I’m repackaging Korean barbecue into a bowl of easy, smoke-free, plant-forward meal.”

"I created this recipe with a helping hand from Meals That Matter, which is a all in one meal planning tool. Having a meal planning service reduced the stress level of weeknight cooking. Especially when the recipes are listing the ingredients that are readily available in my pantry. Moreover, having the ability to easily scale up the portion, just in case I have guests, is so convenient."

Korean Rice Bowl Recipe
4 Servings
Prep Time: 15 minutes Cook Time:1 hour
quick veggie pickles
250 mL 1 cup rice wine vinegar
250 mL 1 cup water
15 mL 1 Tbsp. sugar
15 mL 1 Tbsp. salt
1 carrot, julienned
1 cucumber, julienned
rice bowl
250 mL 1 cup brown rice
5mL 1 tsp Knorr Selects™ Vegetable Bouillon or Knorr Selects™ Chicken Bouillon
30 mL 2 Tbsp. Knorr® Korean BBQ Seasoning Blend
30 mL 2 Tbsp. Hellmann's® Real Mayonnaise
2 in./5 cm fresh ginger, grated on a microplane
4 cloves garlic, grated on a microplane
1 package (454 g) extra firm tofu, cut into 1-in. cubes and patted dry
1 Japanese eggplant, sliced lengthwise
gochujang mayo
90 mL 6 Tbsp. Hellmann's® Real Mayonnaise
15 1 Tbsp. gochujang hot pepper paste
1 clove garlic, grated on a microplane
1
For Quick Veggie Pickles, bring vinegar, water, sugar and salt to a boil in medium saucepan. Simmer until sugar dissolves. Pour into medium bowl; add carrot and cucumber and let stand 1 hour.
2
For Rice Bowl, cook brown rice according to package directions adding Knorr Selects™ Vegetable Bouillon to cooking water.


3
Meanwhile, preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Combine Knorr® Korean BBQ Seasoning Blend, Hellmann's® Real Mayonnaise, ginger and garlic in small bowl, adding a little water if need to form a paste. Coat tofu and eggplant with mixture and roast, 25 minutes or until eggplant is tender.
4
For Gochujang Mayo, combine Hellmann's® Real Mayonnaise, gochujang and garlic in small bowl. Spoon rice, tofu and eggplant mixture and Pickled Veggies into bowls and drizzle evenly with Gochujang Mayo. Garnish, if desired, with toasted sesame seeds, roasted seaweed and sliced green onions.
